Paneer Butter Masala 🧀🍛

Paneer Butter Masala 🧀🍛

🥘 Ingredients

  • 50g Paneer (cubed)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream
  • 1/4 cup Cashew Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar (optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

📌 Procedure

  • Heat oil and butter in a pan on medium flame.
  • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts separating.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Stir in the cashew paste and cook for 2 minutes for a rich texture.
  • Pour water as needed and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Gently add paneer cubes and mix well.
  • Stir in fresh cream for a creamy consistency.
  • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add to the gravy for enhanced flavor.
  • Add garam masala and sugar (optional). Mix everything well and simmer for another 2 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Enjoy your restaurant-style Paneer Butter Masala at home! 😋🔥

Vegetable Biryani

🌿 Vegetable Biryani 🍛

🥕 🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 1.5 cups Mixed Vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, cauliflower, etc.)
  • 1 Onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 Tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup Yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Biryani Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • 5-6 Cashews (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Mint Leaves (chopped)
  • 2 Cups Water
  • Salt (to taste)

📌 Procedure

  • Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  • Heat ghee or oil in a large pan or pressure cooker.
  • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, biryani masala, and salt.
  • Add curd (yogurt) and cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add mixed vegetables and stir well.
  • Pour in 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  • Add the soaked basmati rice and mix gently.
  • Sprinkle fresh mint and coriander leaves on top.
  • Cover and cook on low flame for 15 minutes (or 1 whistle in a pressure cooker).
  • Let it sit for 5 minutes before fluffing the biryani gently with a fork.
  • Serve hot with raita, salad, or pickle.

Enjoy your flavorful Vegetable Biryani! 🍛🔥

Chana Masala

🌱 Chana Masala 🍛

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Chickpeas (soaked overnight or canned)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Amchur (dry mango powder) - optional
  • 1 Green Chili (slit)
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice

🔥 Procedure

  • Soak chickpeas overnight and pressure cook them for 4-5 whistles until soft. If using canned chickpeas, rinse and drain.
  • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until oil starts separating.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add cooked chickpeas and mix everything well.
  • Pour water as needed and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  • Add garam masala, kasuri methi (crushed), and amchur powder. Stir well.
  • Simmer for another 2-3 minutes and turn off the heat.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and drizzle with lemon juice.
  • Serve hot with rice, roti, or naan.

Enjoy your delicious and spicy Chana Masala! 😋🔥

Aloo Gobi

🥔🌼 Aloo Gobi 🍛

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 Potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 1 small Cauliflower (cut into florets)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Powder

  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)

Dal Tadka

🥣 Dal Tadka 🍛

🌿 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Toor Dal (Yellow Split Pigeon Peas)
  • 3 cups Water
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (slit)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 2 Dry Red Chilies
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • 1 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • Salt (to taste)

🔥 Procedure

  • Rinse the toor dal thoroughly and pressure cook with 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt for 3-4 whistles until soft.
  • Mash the cooked dal and keep it aside.
  • Heat ghee or oil in a pan and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and dry red chilies.
  • Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Mix in red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala.
  • Pour the mashed dal into the pan and mix well. Let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add for extra flavor.
  • For extra tadka, heat 1 tbsp ghee in a small pan, add some cumin seeds, chopped garlic, and a dry red chili, then pour this over the dal.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.

Enjoy your comforting Dal Tadka! 😋🔥

Palak Paneer

🌿 Palak Paneer 🧀

🥬 Ingredients

  • 200g Paneer (cubed)
  • 2 cups Spinach (Palak, washed and chopped)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped, optional)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tbsp Fresh Cream (optional, for richness)
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • 1 tbsp Butter or Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/4 cup Water

🔥 Procedure

  • Blanch spinach by boiling it in hot water for 2 minutes, then transferring it to ice water.
  • Blend the spinach into a smooth puree and keep it aside.
  • Heat butter or oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  • Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts separating.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Stir in the spinach puree and let it simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  • Gently add paneer cubes and mix well.
  • Sprinkle garam masala and crush kasuri methi between your palms before adding.
  • For a creamier texture, stir in fresh cream (optional).
  • Simmer for another 2 minutes and turn off the heat.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Palak Paneer! 😋🔥

Vegetable Pulao

🥕 Vegetable Pulao 🍚

🥦 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 1.5 cups Mixed Vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, etc.)
  • 1 Onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 Tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 2 Cloves
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick
  • 2 Green Cardamoms
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • 2 Cups Water
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp Mint Leaves (chopped, optional)

🔥 Procedure

  • Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  • Heat ghee or oil in a pan or pressure cooker.
  • Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, and cardamoms. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell disappears.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  • Add mixed vegetables and stir well.
  • Pour in 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  • Add the soaked basmati rice and mix gently.
  • Sprinkle fresh mint and coriander leaves on top.
  • Cover and cook on low flame for 12-15 minutes (or 1 whistle in a pressure cooker).
  • Let it sit for 5 minutes before fluffing the pulao gently with a fork.
  • Serve hot with raita, pickle, or papad.

Enjoy your fragrant and tasty Vegetable Pulao! 🍛🔥

Bhindi Masala

🌿 Bhindi Masala 🍛

🥒 Ingredients

  • 250g Bhindi (Okra, Ladyfinger) - washed, dried, and chopped
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 Tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (slit)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Dry Mango Powder (Amchur)
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)

🔥 Procedure

  • Wash and completely dry the bhindi before chopping to prevent stickiness.
  • Heat 1/2 tbsp oil in a pan and sauté the chopped bhindi for 5-7 minutes until lightly crispy. Remove and set aside.
  • In the same pan, add the remaining oil and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and green chili. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  • Add the sautéed bhindi and mix well to coat with the masala.
  • Cover and cook on low flame for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Sprinkle garam masala, amchur powder, and crushed kasuri methi. Mix well.
  • Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

Enjoy your flavorful and delicious Bhindi Masala! 🍛🔥

Gobi Manchurian

🌰 Gobi Manchurian 🍜

🥦 Ingredients

For the Batter:

  • 2 cups Cauliflower Florets (Gobi)
  • 1/2 cup All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 1/4 cup Corn Flour
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/4 tsp Black Pepper Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For the Manchurian Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1/2 cup Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 cup Capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 tbsp Spring Onion (chopped, for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Tomato Ketchup
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper Powder
  • 1 tsp Corn Flour mixed with 2 tbsp Water (for thickening)
  • Salt (to taste)

🔥 Procedure

For Frying Cauliflower:

  • Boil water with a pinch of salt and blanch the cauliflower florets for 5 minutes. Drain and pat dry.
  • In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, corn flour, red chili powder, black pepper, salt, and ginger-garlic paste.
  • Add water little by little to make a thick batter.
  • Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter and deep fry until golden and crispy.
  • Drain excess oil and keep the fried gobi aside.

For the Manchurian Sauce:

  • Heat oil in a pan and sauté chopped onions and capsicum on high flame.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Mix in soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, vinegar, black pepper, and salt.
  • Stir in the corn flour slurry (corn flour mixed with water) and let the sauce thicken.
  • Add the fried cauliflower and toss well to coat with the sauce.
  • Garnish with chopped spring onions.
  • Serve hot as a snack or with fried rice/noodles.

Enjoy your crispy and spicy Gobi Manchurian! 🍜🔥

Rajma Curry

🍛 Rajma Curry 🫘

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Rajma (Kidney Beans, soaked overnight)
  • 3 cups Water (for pressure cooking)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)

🔥 Procedure

  • Soak the rajma (kidney beans) overnight in water.
  • Drain and pressure cook with 3 cups of water for 15 minutes (or until soft).
  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Cook until fragrant.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add to the mixture.
  • Pour in the cooked rajma along with its water. Stir and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  • Stir in garam masala and mix well.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.

Enjoy your delicious and comforting Rajma Curry! 🍛🔥

Baingan Bharta

🔥 Baingan Bharta 🍆

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 large Eggplant (Baingan)
  • 2 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 Green Chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)

🔥 Procedure

  • Roast the eggplant on an open flame or in the oven until the skin is charred and soft.
  • Let it cool, then peel off the skin and mash the pulp.
  • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Cook until fragrant.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Stir well.
  • Add the mashed eggplant and mix everything well.
  • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add to the mixture.
  • Stir in garam masala and let it cook for another 2 minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with roti or paratha.

Enjoy the smoky and flavorful Baingan Bharta! 🍆🔥

Stuffed Bell Peppers

🫑 Stuffed Bell Peppers 🍽️

🥘 Ingredients

  • 3 Large Bell Peppers (any color, halved and deseeded)
  • 1 cup Cooked Rice
  • 1/2 cup Mixed Vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, beans, etc.)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 Tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 cup Paneer (crumbled) or Tofu
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1/4 cup Grated Cheese (optional, for topping)

🔥 Procedure

  • Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  • Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5 minutes.
  • Stir in crumbled paneer (or tofu) and cooked rice. Mix well.
  • Fill each bell pepper half with the prepared stuffing.
  • Sprinkle grated cheese on top if desired.
  • Place stuffed peppers in a baking dish and bake for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with yogurt or a side salad.

Enjoy your delicious and healthy Stuffed Bell Peppers! 🫑🔥

Soya Chunks Curry

🌱 Soya Chunks Curry 🍛

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Soya Chunks
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/4 cup Yogurt (curd)
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream
  • 1/4 cup Cashew Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)

🔥 Procedure

  • Boil soya chunks in water for 5 minutes. Drain and squeeze out excess water. Set aside.
  • Heat oil and butter in a pan over medium flame.
  • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts separating.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Stir in the cashew paste and cook for 2 minutes for a creamy texture.
  • Add the boiled soya chunks and mix well.
  • Pour in water and let it simmer for 10 minutes until the soya chunks absorb the flavors.
  • Stir in fresh cream and yogurt for a rich consistency.
  • Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add to the curry.
  • Add garam masala and sugar (optional). Mix well and let it simmer for another 2 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with roti, naan, or jeera rice.
