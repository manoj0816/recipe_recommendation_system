Idli🍚

Idli 🍚🥥

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Idli Rice
  • 1 cup Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils)
  • 1/2 tsp Fenugreek Seeds (Methi)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as required)
  • Oil (for greasing the idli plates)

📌 Procedure

  • Wash and soak the idli rice and urad dal separately in water for 6-8 hours.
  • Soak fenugreek seeds along with urad dal.
  • Grind urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth, fluffy batter using little water.
  • Grind idli rice into a slightly coarse batter.
  • Mix both batters well, add salt, and let it ferment overnight (8-12 hours) until it doubles in volume.
  • Grease the idli plates with oil and pour the batter into each mold.
  • Steam in an idli cooker or steamer for 10-12 minutes.
  • Check with a toothpick—if it comes out clean, the idlis are ready.
  • Remove from the steamer, let cool for a minute, and gently scoop out the idlis.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Enjoy your soft and fluffy Idlis with chutney and sambar! 😋🔥

Dosa🌯

Dosa 🥞🔥

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Idli Rice
  • 1/2 cup Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils)
  • 1/4 cup Chana Dal (Bengal Gram Lentils)
  • 1/2 tsp Fenugreek Seeds (Methi)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as required)
  • Oil or Ghee (for cooking)

📌 Procedure

  • Wash and soak idli rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately in water for 6-8 hours.
  • Grind urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth, fluffy batter using little water.
  • Grind the rice and chana dal into a slightly coarse batter.
  • Mix both batters well, add salt, and let it ferment overnight (8-12 hours) until it doubles in volume.
  • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron dosa tawa on medium flame and grease it lightly.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  • Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and cook until golden brown.
  • Flip if needed and cook for a few seconds.
  • Remove from the tawa and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Enjoy your crispy and delicious Dosa with chutney and sambar! 😋🔥

Poha🍛

Poha 🍛🥔

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Poha (Flattened Rice)
  • 1 medium Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 small Potato (peeled & diced)
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/4 tsp Asafoetida (Hing)
  • 8-10 Curry Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Peanuts
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed)

📌 Procedure

  • Rinse the poha in water for a few seconds, drain it completely, and let it rest.
  • Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and peanuts. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and green chili. Sauté until onions turn soft.
  • Add diced potatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until potatoes are soft.
  • Add curry leaves and asafoetida, mix well.
  • Gently add the soaked poha and mix everything well.
  • Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes on low heat.
  • Turn off the flame, add lemon juice and coriander leaves, and mix well.
  • Serve hot with tea or as a light breakfast.

Enjoy your delicious and healthy Poha! 😋🔥

Upma🥣

Upma 🍛🌿

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Rava (Semolina/Sooji)
  • 2 cups Water
  • 1 small Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1/2 inch Ginger (grated)
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 8-10 Curry Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Chana Dal (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Urad Dal (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Peanuts or Cashews (optional)
  • 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • 1/4 tsp Asafoetida (Hing)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)

📌 Procedure

  • Dry roast the rava in a pan on low flame until it turns slightly golden and aromatic. Set aside.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add cumin seeds, chana dal, urad dal, peanuts/cashews, and sauté until golden.
  • Add chopped onions, green chili, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn soft.
  • Add asafoetida, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Pour in water and bring it to a boil.
  • Slowly add the roasted rava while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  • Cover and cook on low flame for 2-3 minutes until the water is absorbed.
  • Turn off the heat, add lemon juice and coriander leaves, and mix well.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney or pickle.

Enjoy your delicious and healthy Upma! 😋🔥

Paratha🫓

Paratha 🫓🔥

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Whole Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 3/4 cup Water (as needed)
  • 2 tbsp Ghee or Oil (for kneading and cooking)
  • Extra Flour (for rolling)

📌 Procedure

  • In a mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and salt.
  • Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  • Apply 1/2 tbsp ghee or oil, cover, and rest the dough for 15-20 minutes.
  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll them into small balls.
  • Take a ball, dust it with dry flour, and roll it into a circle using a rolling pin.
  • Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium flame and place the rolled paratha on it.
  • Cook for 30-40 seconds until bubbles appear, then flip.
  • Apply ghee or oil on the top side and flip again.
  • Press gently with a spatula and cook until golden brown spots appear.
  • Flip one more time, apply ghee, and cook until fully done.
  • Remove from heat and serve hot with curd, pickle, or butter.

Enjoy your soft and crispy homemade Paratha! 😋🔥

Chole Bhature🍽️

Chole Bhature 🍛🫓

🥘 Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 1 cup Chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Chole Masala (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 2 cups Water
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)

For Bhature (Deep-Fried Bread):

  • 2 cups All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 2 tbsp Semolina (Sooji)
  • 1/2 cup Yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Sugar
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for frying)

📌 Procedure

For Chole:

  • Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with 2 cups of water and a pinch of salt for 4-5 whistles.
  • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili, sauté for another minute.
  • Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  • Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, chole masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  • Sprinkle garam masala and amchur powder, mix well, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and turn off the heat.

For Bhature:

  • In a mixing bowl, add all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, salt, and sugar.
  • Add yogurt and mix well. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough.
  • Cover and let the dough rest for 1-2 hours.
  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll them into balls.
  • Roll each ball into a circular shape using a rolling pin.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan and carefully slide in the rolled bhature.
  • Fry until golden and puffed up, then remove on a paper towel.
  • Repeat for all bhature.

Serve hot Chole with crispy Bhature and enjoy a delicious North Indian feast! 😋🔥

Aloo Puri🍲

Aloo Puri 🍲

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Whole Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 cup Water (as needed)
  • 1 tsp Oil (for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For Aloo Sabzi:

  • 3 Medium-sized Potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 Onion (chopped)
  • 1 Tomato (chopped)
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 cup Water (as needed for gravy)

📌 Procedure

  • For Puri:
  • In a bowl, mix whole wheat flour, salt, and 1 tsp oil.
  • Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Let it rest for 15 minutes.
  • Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into small circles.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the puris until golden brown.
  • Drain excess oil and keep warm.
  • For Aloo Sabzi:
  • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  • Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili, sauté for a minute.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  • Add mashed potatoes and stir well.
  • Pour water as needed for a gravy-like consistency and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Finish with garam masala and fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with crispy puris!

Enjoy your delicious Aloo Puri with pickles and curd! 😋🔥

Medu Vada🍩

Medu Vada 🍩

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils)
  • 1 Green Chili (finely chopped)
  • 1-inch Ginger (grated)
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Curry Leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper (crushed)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

📌 Procedure

  • Wash and soak urad dal in water for 4-6 hours.
  • Drain the water and grind the dal into a thick, smooth batter using very little water.
  • Transfer the batter to a bowl and beat it for 3-4 minutes to make it airy and fluffy.
  • Add chopped green chilies, ginger, coriander leaves, curry leaves, black pepper, cumin seeds, and salt. Mix well.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan over medium flame.
  • Wet your hands, take a small portion of the batter, and shape it into a donut-like vada with a hole in the center.
  • Carefully slide the vadas into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  • Drain excess oil using a paper towel.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Enjoy your crispy and fluffy Medu Vadas with chutney and sambar! 😋🔥

Misal Pav🍞

Misal Pav 🍞🔥

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Sprouted Moth Beans (Matki)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 Tomato (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Goda Masala (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 cups Water
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 cup Farsan (Spicy Mixed Snack)
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1 Lemon (cut into wedges)
  • 4 Pav (Bread Rolls)

📌 Procedure

  • Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  • Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, goda masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add the sprouted moth beans and mix thoroughly.
  • Pour water and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the beans are soft.
  • Adjust the seasoning as per taste.
  • In a serving bowl, pour the prepared misal.
  • Top with farsan, chopped coriander, and a squeeze of lemon.
  • Toast the pav with butter on a hot pan.
  • Serve the hot misal with pav on the side.

Enjoy your spicy and delicious Misal Pav with a side of chopped onions and lemon! 😋🔥

Appam with Stew🍛

Appam with Stew 🍛🥥

🥘 Ingredients

For Appam:

  • 2 cups Raw Rice
  • 1/2 cup Cooked Rice
  • 1/2 tsp Yeast
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Coconut Milk
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed)

For Vegetable Stew:

  • 1 cup Mixed Vegetables (Carrot, Potato, Beans, Peas)
  • 1 Onion (sliced)
  • 1 Green Chili (slit)
  • 1/2 inch Ginger (julienned)
  • 1 cup Coconut Milk
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper Powder
  • 1 tbsp Coconut Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed)

📌 Procedure

For Appam:

  • Wash and soak raw rice for 4-5 hours, then drain.
  • Grind soaked rice with cooked rice, coconut milk, and a little water into a smooth batter.
  • Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water and let it activate for 5-10 minutes.
  • Add the yeast mixture and salt to the batter, mix well, and let it ferment overnight.
  • Heat an appam pan, pour a ladle of batter, swirl the pan to spread it thin on the edges, and cook covered until done.

For Vegetable Stew:

  • Heat coconut oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  • Add curry leaves, green chili, and ginger, sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add onions and sauté until translucent.
  • Add chopped vegetables, turmeric powder, black pepper, salt, and water. Cook until vegetables are soft.
  • Lower the flame and pour in coconut milk. Mix gently and let it simmer for 2 minutes (do not boil).
  • Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh curry leaves.

Serve soft and fluffy Appam with creamy and flavorful Vegetable Stew! 😋🥥🔥

Pesarattu🌿

Pesarattu 🌿🥞

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Green Gram (Moong Dal) (soaked for 4-6 hours)
  • 2 tbsp Rice (optional, for crispiness)
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1/2 inch Ginger (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as required for grinding)
  • Oil (for cooking)

📌 Procedure

  • Wash and soak green gram (moong dal) and rice for 4-6 hours.
  • Drain the water and grind soaked dal with green chili, ginger, coriander leaves, cumin, and salt into a smooth batter. Add water as needed to get a pouring consistency.
  • Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium flame and grease it lightly with oil.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the tawa and spread it in a circular motion like a dosa.
  • Drizzle oil around the edges and cook until golden brown and crisp.
  • Flip and cook for another 30 seconds if needed.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney or ginger chutney.

Enjoy your crispy and protein-rich Pesarattu with chutney! 😋🔥

Thepla🌾

Thepla 🌾🥞

🥘 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Whole Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 cup Fenugreek Leaves (Methi) (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Carom Seeds (Ajwain)
  • 2 tbsp Yogurt (Dahi)
  • 1 tbsp Oil (for kneading)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as required for kneading)
  • Oil/Ghee (for cooking)

📌 Procedure

  • In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, chopped fenugreek leaves, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin seeds, ajwain, yogurt, oil, and salt.
  • Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough.
  • Cover and let the dough rest for 15-20 minutes.
  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a thin round chapati-like disc.
  • Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium flame and place the rolled thepla on it.
  • Cook for a few seconds, then flip and apply oil or ghee on both sides.
  • Cook until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
  • Repeat the process for the remaining dough.
  • Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or tea.

Enjoy your delicious and flavorful Thepla with a side of yogurt or pickle! 😋🔥

Rava Dosa🌮

Rava Dosa 🌮

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Semolina (Rava/Suji)
  • 1/4 cup Rice Flour
  • 1/4 cup All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper (crushed)
  • 1/2 tsp Ginger (grated)
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1 Green Chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 Onion (finely chopped)
  • 5-6 Curry Leaves (chopped)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 1/2 cups Water
  • Oil or Ghee (for cooking)

📌 Procedure

  • In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, rice flour, and all-purpose flour.
  • Add cumin seeds, black pepper, grated ginger, green chili, chopped coriander leaves, chopped onion, curry leaves, and salt.
  • Gradually add water while stirring to form a thin, runny batter.
  • Let the batter rest for 15-20 minutes.
  • Heat a non-stick tawa (griddle) on medium-high flame.
  • Stir the batter well before making each dosa as semolina tends to settle.
  • Pour the batter from the edges to the center in a circular motion—do not spread like regular dosa.
  • Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and let it cook until crispy and golden brown.
  • Flip if needed and cook for a few more seconds.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Enjoy the crispy and flavorful Rava Dosa! 😋🔥

Sabudana Khichdi🌟

Sabudana Khichdi 🌟

🥘 Ingredients

  • 1 cup Sabudana (Sago/Tapioca Pearls)
  • 1 medium Potato (boiled & diced)
  • 1/4 cup Roasted Peanuts (coarsely ground)
  • 1 Green Chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 5-6 Curry Leaves
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Leaves (chopped)

📌 Procedure

  • Rinse sabudana under running water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch.
  • Soak it in enough water for 4-6 hours or overnight until the pearls turn soft.
  • Drain any excess water and mix the soaked sabudana with ground peanuts, salt, and sugar.
  • Heat ghee/oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  • Once cumin crackles, add chopped green chilies and curry leaves.
  • Add boiled, diced potatoes and sauté for 2 minutes.
  • Add the sabudana-peanut mixture and stir well.
  • Cook on low flame for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sabudana turns translucent.
  • Turn off the heat and add lemon juice.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with yogurt or coconut chutney.

Enjoy your light and delicious Sabudana Khichdi! 😋🔥
